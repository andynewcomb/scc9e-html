13.6 Pulse rates. Suppose that resting pulse rates for healthy adults are found to follow a Normal distribution, with a mean of 69 beats per minute and a standard deviation of 9.5 beats per minutes. If Bonnie has a pulse rate of 78.5 beats per minute, this means that

  1. (a) approximately 32% of adults have pulse rates higher than Bonnie’s.

  2. (b) approximately 16% of adults have pulse rates higher than Bonnie’s.

  3. (c) Bonnie’s pulse rate is two standard deviations above the mean.

  4. (d) Bonnie’s pulse rate, when converted to a standard score, would be 1.5.
