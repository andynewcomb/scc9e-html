index number
A number that measures the value of a variable relative to its value at a base period.
base period
A specific time period that is used as a benchmark against which other data is measured. This is essential to making sense of an index number.
fixed market basket price index
An index number for the total cost of a fixed collection of goods and services.
Consumer Price Index
Abbreviated CPI, the government's fixed market basket price index for the collection of all the goods and services that consumers buy.
government statistical offices
Government offices that produce data needed for government policy and decisions by businesses and individuals. The data should be accurate, timely, and free from political interference.
real terms
When all dollar amounts represent the same buying power, even though they may describe different years. Similar terms include &#34;constant dollars&#34; and &#34;real income&#34;.
